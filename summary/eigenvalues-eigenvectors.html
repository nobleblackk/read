Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Say that we have a \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\) and a vector \(\vec{v}\in\mathbb{R}^n\).

Vectors (say \(\vec{x}\)) that satisfies \(A\vec{x}=\lambda \vec{x};\quad\lambda\in\mathbb{C}\) are known as eigenvectors and here \(\lambda\) is our eigenvalue.

\(\bullet\)   \(\text{det}(A - \lambda\mathcal{I})=0\)
\(\bullet\)   A \(n\times n\) matrix will have \(n\) eigenvalues.
\(\bullet\)   Sum of eigenvalues \(=\) Sum of diagonal elements of matrix \(A\).
\(\bullet\)   Product of eigenvalues is the determinant of \(A\).
\(\bullet\)   \(A + \alpha\mathcal{I}\) has eigenvalue \(\lambda+\alpha\).
