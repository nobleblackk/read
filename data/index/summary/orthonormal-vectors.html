Orthonormal vectors

Say we have a \(m\times n\) Orthonormal matrix \(Q\), ith columns of \(Q\) is denotes as \(q_i\). \[ q_i^Tq_j=\left\{\begin{matrix} 0& \text{if }i\neq j\\ 1& \text{if }i=j \\ \end{matrix}\right. \] \(\bullet\)   \(Q^TQ=\mathcal{I}\) \(\Rightarrow\) \(Q^T = Q^{-1}\)


Gram Schmidt

Say we have a \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\), with \(n\) independent columns vectors\(( a_1, a_2, \cdots, a_n )\), Gram Schmidt help us to make that matrix \(A\) an Orthonormal Matrix (say \(Q\)).
Let's denote \(i^{th}\) orthonormal column vectors of \(Q\) as \(q_i\).
\(\displaystyle \vec{a_i}_o=\vec{a_i}-\sum_{k=1}^{i-1} (\vec{q}_k\cdot\vec{a}_i)\vec{q}_k\)    \(\displaystyle \vec{q}_i=\frac{\vec{a_i}_o}{\|\vec{a_i}_o\|} \)
