Projection of vector

\(\bullet\)  Projection on another vector
We can get projection of any vector (say) \(\vec{w}\) on to another vector (say) \(\vec{v}\) by a Projection Matrix (say) \(P\), we can get the projected vector (say) \(\vec{p}\), as \(\vec{p}=P\vec{w}\).
\[P=\frac{\vec{v}\ \vec{v}^T}{\vec{v}^T\vec{v}} \]
\(\bullet\)  Projection onto the column space of a matrix
We can get projection of any vector (say)\(\mathbb{Y}\) onto the column space of matrix (say)\(A\) by a Projection Matrix \(P\), we can get the projected vector (say)\(\widehat{\mathbb{Y}}\), as \(\widehat{\mathbb{Y}}=P\mathbb{Y}\). \[P=\mathbb{A}\left(\mathbb{A}^T\mathbb{A}\right)^{-1}\mathbb{A}^T\]
