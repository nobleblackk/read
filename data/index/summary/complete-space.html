Solving \(A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\)

Say that shape of matrix \(A\) is \(m\times n\) , \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}\).

\(A\vec{x}\) is just a linear combinations of column vector of matrix \(A\), so the resultant of this linear combination must lie in the column space of \(A\). So

\(\bullet\)  \(A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\) is solvable if \(\vec{b}\) lies in the column space of \(A\).

\(\bullet\)  Complete solution of \(A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\) is \(\vec{x} = \vec{x}_p+\vec{x}_n\)
\( \vec{x}_p\) is a particular solution for \(A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\) and, \(\vec{x}_n\) is the null space of matrix \(A\).

So any \(\vec{x}\in\vec{x}_p+\vec{x}_n\) solves \(A\vec{x}=\vec{b}\)
